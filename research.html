Water scarcity mitigation

A Novel Spectral-Stochastic Mathematical Framework for Water Scarcity Mitigation via Physics-Informed Neural Operators

Developed an FNO + SDE + MPC framework for precision irrigation, using a 100k-parameter architecture trained on NASA SMAP satellite data. The framework was validated across 50 global sites with 100 Monte Carlo simulations per site and hardware tested on 20 tomato plants.

Proved 3 original theorems, won the AMS Karl Menger Award (3rd place special award) at ISEF 2026, and submitted 2 arXiv preprints.

Computational nephrology

Predicting Chronic Kidney Disease using Physics-Based Neural Networks

Built physics-informed and physics-constrained neural networks for chronic kidney disease prediction, integrating domain structure directly into the learning process. The work was validated at the University of Louisville under Dr. Barati.

An arXiv submission extends the project into a reproducible research artifact.

Proteomics

Proteomic Analysis of Kidney Proximal Tubules

University of Louisville research internship under Dr. Barati analyzing proteomic data from microdissected kidney proximal tubules in mice exposed to sodium benzoate preservative. The project identified 1,674 proteins and uses computational analysis to understand biological response patterns.

Abstract submitted to the American Society of Nephrology.
